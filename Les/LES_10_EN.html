🇳🇱 Dutch Grammar: Adjective Agreement

📘 1. When the adjective is before the noun (attributive)

When an adjective comes directly before a noun, it usually gets the ending -e.

❗ But be careful: there is only ONE exception where you don’t add -e.

❌ You do NOT add -e only if ALL these 3 conditions are true:

  • the noun is a het-word
  • it is singular
  • there is an indefinite article “een”

✅ If even ONE of these conditions is false → you add -e!

🧠 Logic Table:

het-word? singular? “een”? Add -e?
✅ ✅ ✅ ❌ NO
✅ ✅ ❌ ✅ YES
✅ ❌ ✅ ✅ YES
❌ ✅ ✅ ✅ YES

📘 2. When the adjective is after the verb (predicate)

When the adjective comes after the verb (like is, seems, stays...), you never add -e.

Examples:

  • Het huis is mooi → The house is beautiful
  • De kinderen zijn blij → The children are happy

📘 3. Always add -e in these cases

  • ✅ With a possessive (mijn, jouw, zijn...)
  • ✅ With a demonstrative (dit, dat...)
  • ✅ When the adjective is used as a noun (e.g. de rijke = the rich person)
  • ✅ In the plural, always add -e
